HVAC System Examples
VRF

This post aims to introduce application of Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) in OpenStudio modeling. In some applications, when the requirements of ventilation are not very demanding and the outdoor air flow required is low, it is possible to split the ventilation and conditioning systems. In this scenario, the VRF system is a decentralized system used to heat and cool the spaces using complex vapor compression cycle. A central outdoor unit (condenser) is connected to the indoor units (evaporators) and the refrigerant is driven by inverter driven compressors, which vary the speed by changing the frequency of the power supplied to the compressor. As the compressor speed changes, so do the amount of refrigerant delivered by the compressor. Each indoor unit has its own metering device that sends a demand to the central unit, which delivers the amount of refrigerant needed to meet the requirements of the indoor unit.

As in other applications, the vapor compression cycle is intended to be used for cooling mode. However, as it is well-known, when running in reverse, this cycle also can be used for heating mode. In addition, the VRF system has a special feature: it can reject the heat coming from one space in cooling mode to another in heating mode. These features make the VRF system ideally suited for all applications that have part load requirements based on usage or building orientation. The advantages of this system, among others, are:

  • No ductwork is required
  • Reduced noise levels due to fans and ducts
  • Less maintenance is required; Just changing filters and cleaning coils are required

However, there is a need to consider couple of technical constraints. Examples are:

  • Max height difference between outdoor and indoor units: 360 ft (110 m).
  • Max altogether pipe length: 3,280 ft (1,093 m).
  • Max one loop pipe length: 690 ft (230 m).
  • Max indoor units supplied by one outdoor unit: 48.

Fixing Errors
Vertex Mismatch

One of the common errors in creating geometry using FloorSpaceJS is the vertex size mismatch

** ~~~ ** Defaults, constant throughout the year of (18.0) will be used. ** Severe ** RoofCeiling:Detailed="FACE 215", Vertex size mismatch between base surface :FACE 215 and outside boundary surface: FACE 645 ** ~~~ ** The vertex sizes are 5 for base surface and 4 for outside boundary surface. Please check inputs.

Basically, the error mentions there are inconsistency between the number of vertices for two neighboring faces (Face 215 and Face 645 in this case). One possible reason is due to the addition of space types and thermal zones, this severe error occurs.

A solution to fix this error is to first identify the faces.This face needs to have four vertices in the OSM model. Open the model with two different editors to compare the two mentioned faces in the error. Please refer the below images to gain insight.
